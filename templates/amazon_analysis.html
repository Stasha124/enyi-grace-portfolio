To understand how pricing relates to customer satisfaction, we examined the correlation between Price and User Rating. When we compared Fiction and Non-Fiction, we found interesting differences in both average ratings and pricing. Together, these patterns reveal two distinct consumer mindsets:

  • Fiction readers are often motivated by emotional engagement, entertainment, and narrative immersion. They reward books that make them feel deeply connected to characters and stories.
  • Non-Fiction readers are more likely to be seeking knowledge, guidance, or personal transformation. They are willing to pay a higher price when they perceive the author as credible or expert.
üí° Marketing Insight:

Fiction and Non-Fiction perform differently but both are strong: Fiction slightly leads in satisfaction, while Non-Fiction leads in price and perceived value. This creates a natural segmentation opportunity in Amazon‚Äôs marketing and recommendation strategy.

One key question was whether books with more reviews also have higher (or lower) ratings. The correlation between Total Reviews and User Rating was almost zero (r = ‚àí0.0017), indicating no meaningful linear relationship between how many people review a book and how highly it is rated.

üí° Marketing Insight:

The near-zero correlation between reviews and ratings shows that popularity does not equal quality, but it also does not reduce it. Both niche books with few reviews and mass-market books with thousands of reviews can achieve high satisfaction.
